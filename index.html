Projects

Phone Case
Phone Case

In Maker Lab II, my group and I developed a smartphone case marketed towards those who experience motor dysfunction in their wrists. The purpose of this design was to strengthen users’ grip and increase their confidence in operating a smartphone without requiring any form of assistance.

Proximate
Proximate

My group and I coded and manufactured a working proximity sensor that accurately reflected upon several design concepts learned throughout the Maker Lab I course, such as somesthetic design, “DIY” strategies, and tinkering. Proximate aimed to adhere to a range of senses, including sight, sound, and touch. The purpose of this design was to enhance the user’s visuospatial awareness across different scenarios. For example, being completely aware of your surroundings whilst attempting to reverse-park in a tight garage.

PAMIGA
PAMIGA

The objective of this Maker Lab I project was to develop a comprehensive user manual that explains in detail how to create the ubiquitous paper airplane known as the Nakamura Lock. Working on this project allowed me to develop my graphic design skills, as I had to carefully consider design elements surrounding colour, typography, composition, and visual hierarchy.
